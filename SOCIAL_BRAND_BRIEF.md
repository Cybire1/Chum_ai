# Chum AI — Brand & Social Content Brief

**Purpose:** Everything an AI agent (or social manager) needs to create on-brand graphics and content for Chum AI across TikTok, Instagram, X/Twitter, and YouTube Shorts. Self-contained — no extra context required.

---

## 1. What Chum is (the elevator pitch)

**Chum is your private AI wingman.** Screenshot or paste a dating chat → get **3 replies that actually land**, in your voice. *Decode* tells you if they're into you; *Openers* writes the first line from a bio.

The bigger vision: **one private AI for your whole life** — dating, gym, notes, finance — where everything you tell it is processed in a **sealed enclave** (on the 0G network), so nobody (not even us) can read it.

- **Live today:** the dating **Wingman** (this is what we promote now).
- **Roadmap (don't claim as live):** Coach (gym), Notes, Money.

**Two things make it different:**
1. **Replies that don't suck.** Every other "rizz" app is corny, generic, repetitive. Chum's are witty, specific, in *your* voice.
2. **It can't read your DMs.** Your chats are processed privately and never stored — a real promise no competitor can make.

---

## 2. Who we're talking to

- **Primary:** Gen Z + young millennials (17–28) on dating apps (Hinge, Tinder, Bumble) — the "rizz," "ick," "delulu," texting-anxiety crowd. They share screenshots, love before/afters, live on TikTok.
- **Secondary:** privacy-aware users who hate that apps log everything; and the **crypto / 0G ecosystem** (for the tech/launch story).
- **Mindset to speak to:** "what do I even say back??" — the 10 seconds of panic before replying to a crush.

---

## 3. Brand voice

**Playful, witty, confident — a little cheeky. A great wingman: on your side, makes you look good, never tries too hard.**

- Fluent in Gen Z, but **never corny or cringe** (we're literally the anti-cringe app — our copy must prove it).
- Warm and reassuring on privacy ("your secrets are safe").
- Short, punchy, screenshot-able. One idea per post.
- Lowercase-friendly, emoji-friendly (1–2 max), texting register.
- We tease, we wink, we never lecture.

**Sounds like:** "send the text that gets a 'lol stop 😭', not the one that gets left on read."
**Never sounds like:** "Unlock your dating potential with AI-powered conversation optimization." (corporate = death)

**Words we use:** rizz, wingman, the read, your voice, lands, cooked, left on read, ick, green flag.
**Words we avoid:** "revolutionary," "seamless," "leverage," "optimize," anything that smells like a SaaS deck.

## 4. Visual identity (use these exact specs)

The look is **LIGHT, playful, artistic, modern, gradient-forward.** Bright cream backgrounds, the brand gradient everywhere, soft colorful shadows, big rounded shapes, expressive bold type. Think Cash App × Headspace × a Gen-Z dating app — NOT dark, NOT "techy AI," NOT stock photos.

### Brand gradient (the signature — use it on logos, buttons, badges, key shapes)
`#9B4DF0` (purple) → `#F0438E` (pink/magenta) → `#FF7A45` (orange)
Diagonal (top-left → bottom-right).

### Visual motifs
- **Aurora gradient-mesh backdrops** — soft purple/pink/orange radial washes bleeding across a cream canvas (our hero background; great for posters and story backgrounds).
- **Gradient squircles** — rounded-square badges (radius ~30%) filled with the brand gradient, holding an emoji or icon. (The app icon is a gradient squircle with a chat bubble.)
- **Soft, slightly-colored shadows** (pink/purple glow) under cards — never harsh black drop-shadows.
- **Reply/result cards** — white rounded cards on cream, the actual product output styled beautifully (this is the most shareable asset — see §6).
- **Bold, tight headlines.** Heavy weight (700–800), generous size.

### Typography
- System / **SF Pro**-style geometric sans. Headlines bold/extra-bold and tight; body clean and readable (17px equivalent). Mono only for tiny technical labels.

### Logo
- Wordmark **"Chum"** in dark ink + a small **"AI"** tag, with the gradient **"C" + chat-bubble** mark. Keep the gradient mark prominent on light backgrounds.

### Don't
- ❌ Dark/black backgrounds, neon-on-black "AI/crypto" aesthetics.
- ❌ Stock photos of couples, generic robots, brains, circuit boards.
- ❌ Harsh shadows, cluttered layouts, more than 2 fonts.
- ❌ Literal "pickup line" clip-art / cheesy hearts.

## 5. Content pillars (rotate these)

1. **Rizz in action (the viral engine, ~50%).** Before/after: the dry text you'd have sent vs Chum's reply. Funny, relatable, screenshot-able. "POV: you finally know what to say back."
2. **Decode / the read (~20%).** "She said 'haha okay' — here's what it actually means." Mini psychology, the confidence hit.
3. **Privacy / trust (~15%).** "Other rizz apps read your DMs. Chum literally can't." The grown-up differentiator; builds trust.
4. **Tech / 0G & launch (~10%).** For the crypto/ecosystem audience: "Every reply runs in a private enclave on 0G — verifiable, decentralized AI." Behind-the-scenes, hackathon (Zero Cup) story.
5. **Community / UGC (~5%).** Reposting users' wins, "rate my rizz," polls, replies to comments in-voice.

---

## 6. Formats that win (make these)

- **Before/after reply card** *(the hero asset).* Split: "you" (a dry line) → "Chum" (the witty reply), on a white card with the gradient accent. Anonymized (no real names/faces/handles).
- **"Decode" verdict card** — a chat snippet + a verdict chip ("She's testing you · 72%") + the move. Looks like the in-app result.
- **Result-card carousels** (IG) — 3–5 slides walking a scenario from "left on read" to "got the date."
- **Short vertical video** (TikTok/Reels) — screen-record the app: paste → reveal → copy. The reveal *is* the hook. End on a branded card.
- **Gradient poster / quote cards** — bold one-liner on an aurora background for X/IG.

> The output card is the ad. Always brand it (small Chum mark + handle), always anonymize the other person.
